Finding My Path in Technology & Management

I found my passion for technology and management during an incredible moment at Kode with Klossy, where I was recognized as a “Future Startup Founder.” As a woman in the tech space, it hasn’t always been easy to identify role models, which sometimes makes it hard for me to see myself working in this industry. That recognition changed my perspective—it showed me that I could embody both the entrepreneur I aspire to be and the Software Engineer I had always dreamed of becoming.

Through my experiences at Kode with Klossy, I discovered that problem-solving is my true calling, especially when it comes to women's health awareness. I’ve realized that coding isn’t just about writing code for the sake of it; my motivation lies in harnessing my skills to create solutions that genuinely help people. A project I worked on, called Girls & Cycles, was all about raising awareness around issues that matter to women. This initiative not only provided me with valuable technical experience but also introduced me to the world of Product Management. During this time, I learned how to assess product features critically, deciding which elements to modify based on what would actually benefit users the most.

Currently, as a student at UIUC, I feel energized by the endless opportunities to blend technology with business. I've immersed myself in this environment by joining IBC’s tech team, NOBE’s tech team, and taking on the responsibility of Project Manager for Project:Code. In all these roles, I've been able to marry my technical abilities with practical applications while honing my business skills. I’m always on the lookout for more project management opportunities because I believe that strengthening my blend of technical and business knowledge will better prepare me for future jobs and internships.

Looking forward, I am eager to secure an internship at top companies like Google STEP or Microsoft Explore. I am convinced that rotating through both Software Engineering and Project Management roles will give me a comprehensive understanding of the product lifecycle. These experiences will enable me to figure out where my true interests lie within the broader realms of software and technology, ultimately helping me pinpoint the best roles for me in the future.

After attending various lectures in this class, I've gained an appreciation for the importance of a solid technical foundation before taking on leadership roles or starting new projects. With this realization, I plan to soak up as much information as I can and apply it to my project, Girls & Cycles. My aim is to work in a corporate setting for a while to gain experience before pursuing my dream of launching my own startup. I envision a venture that utilizes technology to enhance women's health awareness and education. A lecture by George Ploss that discussed the possibility of nurturing startup ideas within larger corporate structures really opened my eyes to the fact that while working in a corporation, I can still chase innovative projects beyond conventional coding tasks.

I'm dedicated to using my growing skills and experiences to create meaningful change. I genuinely believe that technology holds immense potential to empower women, and I’m committed to being part of that dialogue. Throughout my journey, one of my primary goals is to inspire others, especially young women, to envision themselves as future leaders in technology and beyond. I want to contribute to a deeper understanding of women’s health issues using technology. I know I have a long way to go, but I'm excited to embrace the challenges ahead and make a difference. Women’s health deserves attention, and I aim to be an advocate for that change.
